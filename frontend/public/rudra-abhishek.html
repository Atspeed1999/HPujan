What is Rudrabhishek

The abhishekam that invokes Shiva in His most benevolent form

Rudrabhishek is the ritual bathing (abhishekam) of the Shivalinga while the Sri Rudram — 11 chapters (Namakam & Chamakam) of the Yajurveda — is recited. It is traditionally performed to dissolve negativity, restore health and harmony, and draw Lord Shiva's protection over a home and family.

Why families choose it

To clear persistent obstacles and Grah Dosh, for healing and peace of mind, before a major life consolidation, or simply to invite Shiva's grace into the home.

The scholarly depth

Sri Rudram demands mastery of the intricate Chamakam pattern. Our scholars have completed traditional Rudra Parayana training spanning several years — so every svara is chanted as the Veda prescribes.

What's included

One transparent dakshina. Nothing hidden.

  • Post-ritual prasad & a 15-minute pre-ritual consultation
How a Rudrabhishek unfolds

From Sankalpa to Aarti

Sankalpa & Purvanga

The intention is set, Ganesha and the Navagraha are honoured, and the space is purified.

Abhishekam with Sri Rudram

The Shivalinga is bathed with Panchamrit, Gangajal and herbs as Namakam & Chamakam are chanted.

Aarti & Prasad

The ceremony concludes with Aarti, blessings (ashirvad), and distribution of prasad to the family.

How long does the ceremony take?
A traditional Rudrabhishek runs about 3 hours, depending on the chosen scale and your Sankalpa.
Do you bring everything needed?
Yes. The dakshina includes all Himalayan Samagri, Panchamrit, Gangajal, the Havan Kund setup and its dismantling. You need only provide a clean space.
What does the dakshina depend on?
It starts from ₹5,100 and varies with the scale of the ceremony, your specific Sankalpa, and the scholar's travel. The exact amount is always confirmed during your free consultation — never any hidden fees.
How do I pick an auspicious date?
During the free consultation, the scholar studies your intention and recommends the right Muhurta. Bookings are typically arranged 5–7 days ahead for Muhurta selection and Samagri sourcing.
